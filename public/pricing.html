FAQ

What is a NIT-ID?
A Node Identity Token — a unique cryptographic identity (Ed25519 keypair) minted for any node: human, hardware, or service. It's the unit of identity in the NIT-IN mesh.
What does "resonance edge" mean?
A resonance edge is a verified connection between two NIT-IDs. Edges have weights that decay and grow based on signal frequency — stronger connections mean higher resonance.
Can I mint a NIT-ID for my Arduino / Raspberry Pi?
Yes. Any device with a unique hardware signature (MAC address, serial number) can mint a NIT-ID via the API. Hardware nodes appear on the network graph alongside human nodes.
What is hub federation?
Hub federation lets multiple NIT-IN hubs share node registries and route signals across hub boundaries. HUB+ plans can connect to any other federated hub on the network.
When will paid plans launch?
HUB, SIGNAL, and ENTERPRISE go live May 19, 2026. Free NODE minting is available now at no cost.
Is my private key ever stored on the server?
No. Your Ed25519 private key is generated server-side at mint, returned once in the response, and never stored. The server holds only your public key. You are responsible for saving your private key.
